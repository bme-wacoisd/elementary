# Teacher Unit Internalization Protocol









Step 1: **Understand the big picture.**

**•** **Use Appendix B located in Unit 1:** cing Guide.
hat they learned in the unit before, and what they will
learn next.

**•** **Use the Table of Contents:** Identify how many days are allocated for lessons, pausing points, and assessments.

**•** **Using the TEKS chart in the Teacher Resources:** Review the TEKS and ELPS being taught. Highlight the TEKS that
are being assessed.

**•** **Use the Scope and Sequence/Pacing Guide provided by your district.** Note which days you will be teaching the
lessons and administering the assessments.

**•** Why is this unit important?

Step 2: **Know your destination.**

**•** **Use the Unit Introduction:** Read the Unit Introduction; discuss the recommendations for delivery and rationale
of instruction for foundational skills; identify the assessment opportunities throughout the unit.

**•** **Use the unit assessment:** Review the unit assessment sections; if applicable, complete the comprehension
assessment as the students would and annotate the foundational skills required to access the text.

**•** How will identifying the skills that students will need to access the text within the unit assessment impact
your daily instruction throughout the unit?

Step 3: **Examine the arc of learning.**

**•** **Use the Table of Contents:** Review how the lessons build on one another; how will students develop the prioritized
skills over the course of the unit? Identify how the lessons connect to the unit assessment. Locate and review the
Pausing Point lessons. Locate and review the Teacher Resources section.

**•** How will you support students who do not exhibit mastery of prerequired skills necessary for the new
learning within the unit?

Step 4: **Organize your resources.**

**•** **Use the Unit Introduction:** Review the Skills Strand Components to identify the additional materials needed for
this unit i.e. markers, index cards, chart paper etc.

**•** **Use the Teacher Resources:** Prepare to use anecdotal recordings. Prepare resources needed for selected activities.

**•** What would you like the Special Education and ESL teacher to do based on what you know about
the unit? How will those actions contribute to improved student outcomes?

**•** **Use the text complexity rubric:** Review the rubric to check the rigor of the text.
