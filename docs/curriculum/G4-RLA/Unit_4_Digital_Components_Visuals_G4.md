**VOCABULARY**


1. Determine the **syllabication** of a word by clapping or tapping syllables.


2. Identify known word parts.


3. Use your knowledge of root words, prefixes, and suffixes to help you determine the **word’s origin** .


4. Use your knowledge of decoding and the word’s origin to help you determine the word’s **pronunciation** .


5. Lastly, use your background knowledge and your understanding of word parts to help you determine the

word’s **meaning** and **part of speech** .


6. After analyzing the word on your own, use the glossary or an online dictionary to check your work.


Appendix | **Unit 4** | **31**


**VOCABULARY - WORD ANALYSIS**


1. Look at the vocabulary word **informative** from Lesson 1.


2. How many syllables are in the word **informative** ?


3. Using your knowledge of root words, prefixes, and suffixes, what is the root word for **informative** ?


4. Using a digital resource, find other words with the root word **info** .


5. Predict what the definition of **informative** is. Then, look it up in the glossary.


Appendix | **Unit 4** | **32**


**VOCABULARY - FOUR PART MODEL**


1. Take out a blank piece of paper and fold it into quarters (a vertical fold and then a horizontal fold).


2. Unfold the paper and write your assigned vocabulary word in the center of the paper.


3. In the upper left quadrant, write your own definition of the word.


4. In the upper right quadrant, draw a picture that describes the word.


5. In the lower left quadrant, write the part of speech.


6. In the lower right quadrant, write a synonym for the word.


7. You may use a digital resource for support.


8. Share your vocabulary organizer with your partner, comparing and discussing definitions, pictures, etc. Be

sure to use correct pronunciation of the vocabulary word.
