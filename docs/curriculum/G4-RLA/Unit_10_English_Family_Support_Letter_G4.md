# **Welcome!**
#### **Grade 4, Unit 10**
## **Novel Study: Number the Stars**

Dear Family Member,


Welcome to Grade 4, Unit 10 Novel Study: _Number the Stars_ . In this unit, students will read the
novel _Number the Stars_ by Lois Lowry and will focus on analyzing character development and
making text-to-life connections. Here is what your student will be learning about and
accomplishing over the next few weeks.

### **Whatâ€™s the story?**

Students will learn about **bravery** and **friendship** as they read _Number the Stars._ This novel
study will help students grow in their reading abilities and their enjoyment of **authentic**
**texts** and will encourage them to appreciate other people's **perspectives** and **experiences** .

### **What will my student learn?**

Students will learn about the **author's craft and purpose** as they analyze the relationships
among the characters in the story. They will be challenged to **evaluate** their definitions of
**heroism** and **bravery**, **reflect** on their relationships, and **integrate** the different kinds of
learning they do throughout the school day. They will gain an understanding of the conflict
between the two alliances during World War II and the threat inherent to a totalitarian
government.


Daily **reading** and **writing activities** to practice **literary analysis** will support students'
successful completion of the culminating task for the unit: the _Number the Stars_ Character
Analysis Project. For this project, students will **create a presentation** that portrays their own
analysis of how two or three characters develop in the novel.

### **Conversation starters**

Ask your student questions about the unit to promote discussion and continued learning:

1. Why does the author write about bravery?

**Follow up:** Do you think everyone can be brave? What are the characteristics of bravery?
2. Who are the main characters in the novel?

**Follow up:** Which character or characters do you like the most? What do you find
interesting about them?

3. Why is it good to be kind to people who are different from you?

**Follow up:** How can you show consideration to someone who has different beliefs than
you?


4. Can you relate to any of the characters in the story?

**Follow up:** How does Annemarie show bravery? How does she protect others?
