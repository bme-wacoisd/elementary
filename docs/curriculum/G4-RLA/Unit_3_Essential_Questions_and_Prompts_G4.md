# Grade 4
## Unit 3: Contemporary Fiction: Letters from Heaven

Use the Interactive Read-Aloud lesson and text on the following page to kick off this
unit with your students.

### Unit-Level Essential Question
#### How can a story’s literary elements help readers make inferences about characters, themes, and messages in the story?


Lessons 1–4


Guiding Question: How do you determine a theme of a story?


Writing Prompt: What themes appear in the excerpts you have read so far?
Use details from the text to support your response.


Lessons 5–6


Guiding Question: How and why do authors use sensory and figurative
language in their writing?


Writing Prompt: How does the author’s use of sensory language
and figurative language in _Letters from Heaven_ enhance the reader’s
understanding of the story?


Lessons 7–10


Guiding Question: How do characters and their relationships change
in a story?


Writing Prompt: How did Celeste’s grandmother’s letters contribute
to changes in both Mami and Celeste? How did the letters impact their
relationship?

Lessons 11–13


Guiding Question: How can we identify the author’s purpose for writing and their message(s)
in a story?


Writing Prompt: What is the message that the author is trying to convey in _Letters from Heaven_ ?
How is this supported by what the characters learned in the story?


Unit 3 Culminating Activity


The themes of relationships and food emerge throughout the story _Letters from Heaven._ Do you
think Celeste will continue to use food as a way of keeping her friends and family close to her
throughout her life? Write a short narrative that picks up the story twenty years later, when Celeste
is a young woman. In the scene, show whether she has continued her grandmother’s traditions and
learned from the lessons her grandmother taught her.
